前言：方法论纠偏

❌ 第一版（已废弃）的错误

  1. 把 Aralia / johnlocke.org.cn 等非官方网站 的总结直接当成官方 Rubric
  2. 用 AI 自己生成的范文 代替官网获奖文章做校准
  3. 没有去官网 www.johnlockeinstitute.com 做任何验证

结论：自己出题、自己答卷、自己打分——毫无意义。

✅ 第二版（当前）的正确方法

原则：先找官方 Rubric → 没有则从官网获奖范文推断 → 用真题验证 → 后续用晋级/非晋级论文校准逼近真相。

Step操作数据来源
1官网调研是否有公开 Rubricjohnlockeinstitute.com 官网 FAQ
2找到并下载官网真实获奖范文官网公开的 PDF 文件（3 篇）
3对范文做 21 维特征提取项目自带校准引擎
4从特征 + 官网描述反推 Rubric标注为 假设性 / MEDIUM 置信度

Phase 1：官网调研 — 寻找官方 Rubric

1.1 调研目标

访问 www.johnlockeinstitute.com/essay-competition，查找官方公开的评审标准文档。

1.2 关键发现

发现原文/来源结论
没有公开 Rubric "Our grading system is proprietary." 无法从官网获取详细评分表，需从范文反推
有 7 项评判描述 "Essays will be judged on knowledge and understanding of the relevant material, the competent use of evidence, quality of argumentation, originality, structure, writing style and persuasive force." 这 7 项可以作为 Rubric 的 一级维度框架（但各维度权重未知）
格式规定详细 2000 词限制、不接受脚注、接受文内引用和尾注、MLA/APA/Chicago 均可、允许 AI 辅助但过度依赖会降低评分 这些是确定信息，可直接写入 Rubric
官网公开获奖范文 官网的 /_files/ugd/ 目录下直接托管历年获奖 PDF 这是推断 Rubric 的 最佳数据来源

3.2 结构性共性（3 篇 100% 一致的模式）

  1. 开篇 Hook：均以一个生动的具体案例/叙事开头（Chateaubriand/Blackstone/Mahabharata Arjuna）
  2. 定义段：专门定义核心概念（epistemic peer / public censure / presentism）
  3. Thesis 前置：引言末句明确陈述论点（"This essay argues that..."）
  4. 反方论点独立段落：不回避最强反对论，正面呈现后再 rebuttal
  5. 跨学科引用：均混合使用哲学经典 + 法律判例 + 学术期刊 + 当代学者
⚠ 注意：特征提取工具的 citation_density 对 MLA 风格的部分范文未检测到正文引用标记，这是因为获奖范文使用了叙述性引用（如 "Aristotle identifies..."）而非括号引用，系统暂时不支持此种格式。

Phase 4：反推假设性 Rubric（带置信度标注）

4.1 Rubric 设计逻辑

因为官网没有公开 Rubric，我们使用"官网描述 + 获奖范文特征"两种信息来源反推。每个维度标注信息来源和置信度，明确区分"确信的"和"需要验证的"。

#维度权重置信度信息来源
1知识理解与深度20%HIGH官网明确提及 + 范文术语使用密度高
2论证质量25%HIGH官网明确提及 + 范文 claim 密度 5–11 个
3原创性与独立思辨20%MEDIUM官网提及但难以量化，需更多样本
4证据使用能力15%HIGH官网明确提及 + Law范文引用14条权威来源
5写作风格与说服力10%MEDIUM官网提及 persuasive force，但主观性强
6结构与清晰度5%HIGH官网提及 + 范文结构一致性 100%
7格式规范与学术诚信5%HIGH官网 FAQ 明确规定了多项格式要求

4.2 关键判定：权重如何决定？

各维度权重无法从官网或范文直接读出，目前的方案为合理假设——将"论证质量"和"原创性"作为 John Locke 的核心区分项赋以较高权重（各 20%–25%），结构与格式作为基本门槛赋以较低权重（各 5%）。权重将在后续校准阶段通过 Cohen's d 修正。

7.3 逐段标注反馈（节选）

段落 2 — Introduction / Hook A3 false_dichotomy

In 2023, an autonomous vehicle operated by Cruise, General Motors' self-driving subsidiary, struck and dragged a pedestrian in San Francisco, prompting California regulators to revoke the company's operating permit. The question of who bore moral responsibility—the algorithm, the engineers, the corporate executives, or the vehicle itself—proved far more complex than any existing regulatory framework could accommodate. This essay argues that machines, regardless of their sophistication, cannot bear moral responsibility in any philosophically meaningful sense, and that the temptation to attribute agency to autonomous systems represents a dangerous abdication of human accountability. Drawing on the philosophical tradition of moral agency, contemporary work in machine ethics, and the legal doctrine of vicarious liability, I contend that responsibility must always terminate with human actors—whether designers, deployers, or regulators.

⚡ false_dichotomy — 假两难推理
"你可以说 Machines are not moral agents in the full Aristotelian sense, but they may be moral patients in a derivative sense. The question is not binary but scalar."

段落 3 — Definition & Framework A3 weak_analogy

Clarity requires distinguishing several concepts that are frequently conflated in public discourse. Moral responsibility, as employed in this essay, refers to the capacity of an agent to be answerable for its actions... An earthquake is causally responsible for the destruction of a city, but no one would suggest prosecuting tectonic plates. Similarly, a self-driving car is causally responsible for a collision, but this does not entail that the car is morally responsible... As the legal philosopher HLA Hart observed, the attribution of responsibility presupposes the capacity to be guided by rules... A thermostat adjusts its behaviour in response to temperature, but no one would say it follows a rule.

⚡ weak_analogy — 弱类比
"A thermostat follows a simple rule. An AI system follows complex learned patterns that approximate rule-following, but it does not understand the rule as a rule. The difference is one of degree of complexity—but that degree may be morally relevant."

段落 4-5 — 重复检测 A2 内容重复

第 4 段结尾："When we blame the algorithm, we let the engineer off the hook. When we fine the corporation that deployed an unsafe system, we create incentives for safer systems."

第 5 段开头："A more nuanced resolution becomes possible when we recognize that the question 'who is responsible?' need not have a single answer."

🔄 两段都在回应"如何分配责任"
修复：删掉第 4 段结尾的实用主义论证部分，将其完全整合到第 5 段。第 4 段只保留对 Dennett 意向立场的反驳。

Thesis Coaching — 当前 thesis vs 更强版本

当前Thesis 完全排除了机器承担任何道德责任的可能——"cannot bear moral responsibility in any philosophically meaningful sense"
脆弱点如果反方提出"如果未来 AI 具备意识怎么办？"或"严格责任（无过错责任）如何解释？"，这个 thesis 会显得过于绝对
更强版本"This essay argues that, given the current and foreseeable state of AI technology, machines cannot bear moral responsibility in the philosophically robust sense required for blame and punishment. While future developments may challenge this conclusion, the present temptation to attribute moral agency to autonomous systems represents a dangerous abdication of human accountability that existing legal frameworks are better equipped to address."

Counterargument Analysis

当前反方强度moderate — 有 counter 但没推到最强
未回应的最强反方"如果 AI 系统能够通过图灵测试，并且其决策过程在功能上等同于人类道德推理（能够给出理由、权衡道德原则、调整行为回应批评），那么否认其道德主体地位就是物种歧视 (speciesism)。为什么神经元 vs 硅基电路应该成为道德地位的判准？"
Rebuttal 结构指导先承认反方说对了哪一半（AI 确实能模拟道德推理）→ 指出关键点不成立（模拟 ≠ 拥有，正如天气预报模拟飓风 ≠ 飓风有意图）→ 回到 thesis（责任必须终止于人类）
字数建议counterargument 150-200 词，rebuttal 200-250 词
